Image Name Timeline Key Accomplishments
Jose Rizal
  1. June 19, 1861 - Rizal was born
  2. March 21, 1887 - Rizal released Noli Mi Tangere
  3. September 18, 1891 - Rizal released El Filibusterismo
  4. December 30, 1896 - Rizal's execution and death

Rizal initiated reforms through words, not violence. He founded La Liga Filipina and published Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, which spearheaded Filipino awareness and nationalism during a time it was frowned upon.
Mariano Gomez
  1. August 2, 1799 - Gomez was born
  2. February 17, 1872 - Gomez was executed under sedition and treason

Mariano Gomez was part of the famous GomBurZa, a group of three secular priests who were indignantly executed. He established the La Verdad, a newspaper that aimed to spread action. He was the oldest of the three.
Jose Burgos
  1. February 9, 1837 - Burgos was born
  2. February 17, 1872 - Burgos was executed under sedition and treason

Jose Burgos was also part of the GomBurZa, being the youngest of the three. With his life, he defended the native clergy and sought for a higher Filipino perspective.

Jacinto Zamora
  1. August 14, 1835 - Zamora was born
  2. February 17, 1872 - Zamora was executed under sedition and treason

Jacinto Zamora was also part of the GomBurZa, being in between the three in terms of age. The Spaniards took advantage of evidence obtained from Zamora's gambling habits, and framed him utilizing that.
Teodoro Agoncillo
  1. November 9, 1912 - Agoncillo was born
  2. Janury 14, 1985 - Agoncillo died

A renowned Filipino historian who published multiple books, with some notable and famous ones being "History of the Filipino People" and "The Revolt of the Masses: The Story of Bonifacio and the Katipunan".
